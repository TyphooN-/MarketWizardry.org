Frequently Asked Questions

What is VaR and why should I care about my financial doom?

VaR (Value at Risk) estimates the maximum loss your position might suffer over a specific time period. Think of it as a mathematical crystal ball that tells you exactly how much money you're about to lose with 95% confidence. Perfect for masochists who enjoy quantifying their financial suffering.

How does the Position Size Calculator work?

Our calculator offers three risk modes: Stop Loss (traditional risk), VaR (statistical risk), and ATR (volatility-based risk). Choose your preferred method of calculating exactly how many shares you can afford to lose. It's like a GPS for financial destruction - precise directions to your destination.

What's ATR and why does it matter for stop losses?

ATR (Average True Range) measures market volatility over different timeframes. Using ATR for stop losses is like wearing a helmet sized perfectly for the incoming financial brick wall. It adapts to market conditions so your stops aren't too tight (death by a thousand cuts) or too loose (death by one big cut).
